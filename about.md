My name is Ángel A. Morán Ledezma. I was born and raised in Ecatepec, Estado de México.
I am currently completing my PhD at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), in the
Department of Civil Engineering, Geo and Environmental Sciences. My Master’s degree was in
pure mathematics, algebraic geometry, at CINVESTAV, Mexico City.

## The shape of a tree

My mathematical work is organised around a single guiding idea I call *the shape of a tree*.
A tree — whether as an object in graph theory or as the topological representation of an
ultrametric space — encodes the geometry of a surprising number of natural phenomena.

In **biology**, the evolutionary history of a species is represented by a tree: time flows
from the root, and branching events mark the **divergence of lineages**; this object is called
a **phylogenetic tree**. In **physics**, tree-like hierarchical structures capture the energy
landscape underlying the dynamics of proteins and glasses; the object representing such energy
structure is referred to as a **disconnectivity graph**. In **network science** — the mathematics
of pairwise relationships, from social interactions to disease spreading to brain connectivity —
a ubiquitous phenomenon known as **community structure** organises nodes into nested hierarchies:
communities within communities, all the way down. Finding those communities is equivalent to
recovering the tree that encodes the hierarchy. **River networks** offer yet another natural
instance of the same branching geometry.

The tools I bring to these questions are drawn from the theory of stochastic processes
(continuous-time Markov chains), heat kernels, and spectral geometry — a framework for
understanding geometric objects through the spectrum of an associated operator, which I find
to be one of the most beautiful and interesting parts of mathematics.

## Art and science

Like many thinkers before me, the bridge between art and science has been a lifelong
fascination. Understanding human experience through psychology, and how this interplay between
inner experience and the world can be represented in the arts, is for me the best way I could
describe what I do as a photographer and a writer.
